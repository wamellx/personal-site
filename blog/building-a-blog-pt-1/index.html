Building a Blog - Pt. 1

Maxwell Mosson-Baum September 17, 2024

Welcome back!! Day 2 of being fun-employed!

So I’m building a blog. In fact, it’s this blog! This one that you’re reading! This is my second post!

Why? There are enough voices on the internet.

A couple of reasons:

  1. Maybe someday I’d also like to be one of those voices on the internet!
  2. More importantly, I’ve always wanted to be one of the professionals with their own website and a portfolio.

Eventually this page will start to morph into a more static resume/portfolio page - but for now, it’s blog city.

We’re gonna be talkin’ about projects, maybe I’ll do some journaling, maybe if I can buy some new toys I’ll do a product review or something. BUT we gotta start with the actual blog.

Step 1: How the hell do I get a website?

This part is actually pretty easy, and if you’ve watched any YouTube video ever, you probably know what to do. There are only two steps…

  1. Buy a domain
  2. Figure out some a hosting provider

Buying a domain is pretty easy - Google “buy a domain” and pick a provider. In the past I’ve recommended Google Domains, but they got bought by SquareSpace so I guess I recommend SquareSpace now? GoDaddy, BlueHost, NameCheap, SquareSpace all seem fine. Just pick the one that makes you feel happy.

Look at those. I wonder what the CPQ is on these keywords…..

On those sites you can submit your desired name, and it will spit out a bunch of options and prices. Lucky for me, I already had maxwellbaum.com so I get to skip this step!

Domains can range anywhere from like $12/year to $100/yr+ - really depending on which Top-Level Domain you choose (.com, .biz, .info, etc etc etc)

Step 2: Picking a hosting provider

A hosting provider is the company that is actually storing and serving up your website files. A domain name is like a phone number, the hosting provider is the person being called.

Just like a domain, there are a million different options for hosting providers - some just provide a place to put your files, others have full on web-builders.

For now I chose Notion but I’ll probably end up moving to something based off of WordPress eventually

Notion as a CMS (Content Management System)

Pros:

  • I already pay for it (so basically free!)
  • Relatively simple to use (and in a past life I was a Notion Power User)
  • Quick!!! I was up and running in like 2 minutes
  • Love it for Project Management (which, I’m doin a lot of projects right now)

Cons:

  • Where the fuck are the settings
  • Dude it so hard to keep track of all my pages and drafts.
  • Can’t really do much personalization
  • Have to pay extra to use customized domains

Basically - Notion is great because you can just write and go. It’s not great because there’s literally no customization beyond that - but that’s what we like to call a “Future Max Problem”.

I’m a real perfectionist which keeps me from getting things done, to get around it I just have to force myself to ship as early and as often as possible. That means many many MVPs (Minimum Viable Products)

Notion great for an MVP - I can write and get things out of my head and just publish and share right away.

—

V1 is here!

For V1 I made sure I had two things:

  1. A little snippet about me.
  2. A first post.

For the post I used a Notion Database and set it up in Gallery View. This allows for some level of searching and sorting, and gives each post a nice lil picture preview.

Notion DBs allow you to name a ton of different properties (just like you’d do an Excel file or something) - and there are some advanced features that I’ll need to dive into to see if I can use it to automate some updates or publishing.

But for now, I write and copy/paste into the Blog DB and then it’s live!

JK! We’re on V1.1 now!!!

I’ve added some extra pages - About, Experience, Blog, Projects, Contact Me

About → Just a lil bit about me

Experience → My resume with some deep dives

Blog → The blog database

Projects → A portfolio of my (soon to be) finished projects)

Contact Me → My email, my LinkedIn, and a Hubspot form that collects email addresses for future newsletter subscribers!

I think the Hubspot back-end is going to be a whole post in and of itself - all the form does is collect emails. I haven’t gotten done with the actual “email sending” part of it. But that’s for another day!
